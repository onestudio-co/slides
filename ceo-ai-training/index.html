N — NAVIGATE

Governance & Trust

Key Questions for Every AI Workflow
Who is accountable when AI makes a mistake?
AI doesn't replace accountability. Define the human owner for every AI-driven process — not "the AI team."
How transparent are AI decisions to stakeholders?
Can you explain how the AI reached its recommendation? If not, the process needs a human checkpoint.
When does a human intervene?
Define explicit escalation triggers: thresholds, edge cases, sensitive topics, and high-stakes decisions.
What data goes in, and who sees it?
Data classification, access controls, and privacy by design. Non-negotiable in Saudi regulatory context.
🇸🇦
Saudi Regulatory Context
PDPL (2023) — Personal Data Protection Law governs data collection, processing, and storage
SDAIA Guidelines — Saudi Data & AI Authority sets ethical AI principles
CITC Regulations — Telecommunications requirements for AI in communications
Sector-specific — SAMA (finance), MOH (health), MOE (education) have additional AI requirements
Output: A governance checklist tailored to your workflow: accountability map, escalation triggers, data classification, and compliance requirements.

T — TRACK

Measure What Matters

Leading Indicators (Act Now)
⏱️
Cycle Time
Time from trigger to completion — the first metric to improve
🔄
Handoff Count
Number of human-to-human transfers per cycle
📥
Queue Depth
Items waiting for human action at any given time
🤖
Automation Rate
% of steps handled without human intervention
Lagging Indicators (Validate Over Time)
❌
Error Rate
Mistakes per 100 cycles — should decrease with AI assistance
💰
Cost-to-Serve
Total cost per workflow completion — staff time + tools
📊
Capacity
Volume handled per period without adding headcount
⚡
Decision Speed
Time from data-available to decision-made
Exercise: Select 3-5 KPIs from these categories that fit your specific workflow. Define the baseline and target.
